# Hook Patterns

Formula patterns for generating fresh hooks. The agent uses these patterns when adapting trending research or generating fallback hooks from the playbook.

---

## Question Hooks

Ask something that demands an answer. Drives comments and saves.

- "Would you [verb] this [noun]?"
- "Would you pick this home or that one?"
- "Would you change anything about this kitchen?"
- "Would you keep this backyard or renovate?"

## POV Hooks

Put the viewer inside a scenario. Creates emotional connection.

- "POV: [scenario the viewer relates to]"
- "POV: house hunting in Scottsdale for the first time"
- "POV: you moved to Gilbert from Chicago and this is your backyard"
- "POV: your agent sends you this listing at midnight"
- "POV: walking into your dream home for the first time"

## Number Hooks

Structure creates curiosity. Viewers want to see all three (or five).

- "Three things I love about [subject]"
- "Three things I love about this $900K home in Scottsdale"
- "Three features you won't believe at this price"
- "Three reasons this neighborhood is underrated"
- "Five things to know before moving to Gilbert"

## Challenge Hooks

Invite judgment. Viewers love to rate things.

- "Rate this [room/home/feature] 1 to 10"
- "Rate this kitchen 1 to 10"
- "Rate this backyard setup for summer entertaining"
- "Rate this closet. Be honest"
- "Dream home or pass?"

## Hot Take Hooks

Opinions drive engagement more than facts. Be specific.

- "Unpopular opinion about [market/neighborhood/trend]"
- "Unpopular opinion about Scottsdale real estate"
- "The biggest mistake I see buyers making at $1M"
- "Nobody talks about this in Gilbert real estate"
- "This is why [common assumption] is wrong"

## Reveal Hooks

Build anticipation. Works best with cinematic content.

- "Wait for it..."
- "This home has a surprise at the end"
- "You won't believe what's behind this door"
- "I wasn't expecting this when I walked in"

## Relocation Hooks

Specifically for targeting out-of-state movers to Arizona.

- "Stop scrolling if you're thinking about moving to [place]"
- "Stop scrolling if you're thinking about moving to Arizona"
- "Here's what [price] gets you in [city] right now"
- "The one thing about [city] that surprises every transplant"
- "I get asked about [city] every single day. Here's why"

## Curiosity Hooks

Tease something specific without revealing it immediately.

- "The [noun] in [city] that everyone asks me about"
- "The neighborhood in Gilbert everyone asks me about"
- "I found something cool on my walk today"
- "My client's reaction when they saw this home"
- "This might be the best [feature] I've ever shown"

---

## Hook Writing Rules

1. **First 1 to 3 seconds only.** If the hook takes longer to say, it's too long
2. **Specific beats general.** "$1.1M home in Scottsdale" is stronger than "amazing home"
3. **Questions outperform statements** at smaller account sizes
4. **Numbers create structure** that viewers commit to watching through
5. **Never start with "Hey guys" or "So..."** because that's where thumbs scroll past
6. **Test the scroll test:** would you stop scrolling for this hook? If not, rewrite it
